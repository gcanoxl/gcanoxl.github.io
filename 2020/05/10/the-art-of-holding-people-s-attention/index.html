From Magicians to Teachers - The art of holding people's attention

2020-05-10

The importance of holding people’s attention

There are many jobs that people, want to live up to it, have to hone the skills in holding listeners’ or watchers’ attention.

For instance, as a magician, if audiences are able to see whatever you did on the stage, both things you do expect people to know or you do not. The show will wind up with an embarrassing flop.

Or as a teacher, if you’re droning on and on the wonder of the discipline you teach while your students are sleeping or talking. Their scores on the final will tell you how bad your lectures are.

Consequently, we found that magicians and teachers are similar in some points. And maybe, we can make an interesting connection between these two professions.

Just an experimental idea

About 1 year and a half ago, I’ve learned an interesting tip from a lecture of a Chinese great(or maybe greatest) magician called Louis Liu on the topic of “the acting art of cheating” in Beijing Film Academy.

That is "Big movement covers small one". It means that a qualified magician usually cast a big movement(like jumping, gesticulating, etc) to audiences to catch their attention when he braces for doing something audiences should not know.

I was inspired by the lecture. Considering what if a teacher does as much “big movements” as he could, to catch students’ attention?

I have to confess that I really don’t know.

However, I hope if you are a teacher, you want to apply the idea in your class. And to see what happens in your class, if it is possible, tell me with a commentary.
